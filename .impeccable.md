## Design Context

### Users
The primary users are the product development team: product owners, scrum masters, software developers, and QA testers. They use Clairio during weekly planning, daily execution, sprint tracking, release check-ins, and cross-functional handoffs. The core job is to move work from backlog to release with less ambiguity, clearer ownership, and faster decisions.

### Brand Personality
Clairio should feel high-quality, analytical, and confident. The tone should read like an intelligence platform for delivery operations: calm under pressure, precise, and trustworthy. It should feel more like a premium operating console than a cheerful productivity toy.

### Aesthetic Direction
Use Elementus as the visual benchmark for overall posture: dark-forward, institutional, sharp, data-led, and restrained. Borrow its sense of trust, hierarchy, and premium enterprise polish, but adapt the language to product delivery instead of blockchain intelligence. Avoid generic SaaS gradients, bubbly task-app styling, or playful project-management aesthetics.

### Design Principles
1. Put operational clarity ahead of decoration. Users should understand release state, sprint posture, and task movement at a glance.
2. Make the shell feel executive and technical at the same time. It should support both planning leadership and delivery execution.
3. Use contrast, spacing, and typography to create hierarchy before adding more components.
4. Treat boards, tasks, and reporting as one unified command surface instead of separate demo views.
5. Keep interactions sober and precise. Motion, color, and accents should reinforce signal, not add noise.
